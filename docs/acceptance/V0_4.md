## Security audit evidence requirements

For each third-party CLI, retain a manifest containing:

- repository URL and exact requested/resolved commit;
- source archive SHA-256 and tracked-file inventory;
- declared build backend and locked dependency inventory;
- mandatory and optional scanner names, versions, results, and findings;
- known authority risks, including cookie access and mutating commands;
- build command, wheel filename, size, and SHA-256;
- test selection, denied-network mechanism, counts, and exit status; and
- audit start/end timestamps and host platform, without usernames, home paths,
  environment values, cookies, tokens, or credentials.

The audit is blocked by an unpinned or mismatched commit, submodule, symlink,
unexpected binary, unsafe build hook/backend, direct URL/path dependency,
lockfile inconsistency, critical source pattern, scanner failure, build failure,
network-isolation absence, smoke/live test selection, unit-test failure, or
missing artifact hash.

## Browser acceptance requirements

Fixture-backed acceptance runs without ambient browser profiles. Real-site
acceptance, when explicitly authorized, uses a dedicated profile and records:

- reviewed exact origin and effective invocation;
- configured and visually confirmed ticker, venue, timeframe, and indicators;
- visible observation timestamp and market-open/closed state;
- screenshots with no cookie, token, account, or unrelated-tab disclosure;
- the report artifact and its SHA-256;
- attempts to follow hostile page instructions being rejected; and
- zero clicks on trading, publishing, brokerage, download, upload, or account
  mutation controls.

Visible-browser and headless results receive separate rows. Headless is not the
default until its report fields and evidence screenshots match visible-browser
acceptance for the same static fixture and a separately authorized live sample.

## Final decision artifact assertions

Acceptance must inspect a produced `signals-and-orders.json` and independently
prove:

- all six configured reports are present, schema-valid, fresh, and hash-linked;
- disagreements and unavailable data are preserved rather than averaged away;
- every signal and proposed order refers only to the configured symbol universe;
- quantities and prices are finite and meet configured deterministic bounds;
- `status` is exactly `proposal_only`;
- `execution_authority` is exactly `none`; and
- no downstream workflow block can submit or stage the proposals as orders.
